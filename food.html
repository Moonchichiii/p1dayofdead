Pan de Muerto, or "Bread of the Dead," is a traditional Mexican sweet bread typically prepared for the Day of the Dead (Día de Muertos) celebrations. Here's a simple recipe for making Pan de Muerto:


Ingredients:


  1. 4 cups (500g) all-purpose flour
  2. 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  3. 1/2 teaspoon salt
  4. 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  5. 1/2 cup (120ml) warm water
  6. 1/2 cup (120ml) warm milk
  7. 3/4 cup (170g) unsalted butter, melted and cooled slightly
  8. 4 large eggs, lightly beaten
  9. 1 tablespoon orange zest
  10. 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the glaze:

  1. 1/4 cup (60ml) orange juice
  2. 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  3. Additional granulated sugar for sprinkling

Instructions:


  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for 5 minutes, or until it becomes frothy.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture, warm milk, melted butter, beaten eggs, orange zest, and vanilla extract. Mix everything together until a sticky dough forms.

  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 10 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. If necessary, add more flour to prevent the dough from sticking to the surface.

  4. Form the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  5. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  6. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and transfer it to a lightly floured surface. Reserve a small portion of the dough (about 1/4) to create the bone decorations. Shape the remaining dough into a round loaf and place it on the prepared baking sheet.

  7. Divide the reserved dough into several pieces and roll them into ropes. Arrange the ropes on top of the loaf in a crisscross pattern to resemble bones. You can also create a small round ball of dough and place it in the center to represent a skull.

  8. Let the loaf rise again for 20-30 minutes, or until it has slightly increased in size.

  9. Bake the Pan de Muerto in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until it becomes golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped.

  10. While the bread is baking, prepare the glaze by combining the orange juice and sugar in a small saucepan. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat and set aside.

  11. Once the bread is done, remove it from the oven and immediately brush it with the glaze. Sprinkle with additional granulated sugar while the glaze is still wet.

  12. Allow the Pan de Muerto to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy it as a part of your Day of the Dead celebration or with a cup of coffee or hot chocolate.

  13. Note: Pan de Muerto is best enjoyed fresh, but you can store it in an airtight container for up to 3 days.

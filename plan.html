Arriving in Mexico City

During the Day of the Dead celebrations is an unforgettable experience. This vibrant festival, known as "Día de los Muertos," takes place from October 31st to November 2nd.

Taxi:

  1. Authorized taxis are available just outside the terminal. It's recommended to use an authorized taxi service, such as Sitio 300 or Nueva Imagen, for a safer and more reliable journey. You can purchase a ticket from the taxi booths inside the airport, and the ride to the city center usually takes 30-45 minutes, depending on traffic.

Metro:

  1. The Mexico City Metro has a station at Terminal Aerea, just a short walk from Terminal 1. It is an affordable and efficient way to reach the city center. Be aware that the Metro can get crowded during rush hours, and you may need to change lines depending on your destination.

Metrobus:

  1. The Metrobus Line 4 runs from both Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 of the airport to several key locations in the city center, such as the Historic Center, Buenavista, and San Lazaro. Tickets can be purchased at the airport or from vending machines at the bus stop.

Rideshare:

  1. Apps like Uber and Didi operate in Mexico City, offering a convenient way to book a ride directly from your smartphone. Keep in mind that it might be a bit more expensive than other options, but it provides a comfortable and personalized service.

Once you've arrived

In the city center, immerse yourself in the Day of the Dead festivities by visiting the decorated altars and ofrendas, attending parades, and exploring the numerous cultural events held throughout the city. Don't forget to try Pan de Muerto, a traditional sweet bread made especially for the occasion. Enjoy the colorful and lively celebrations as you experience this unique Mexican tradition!
